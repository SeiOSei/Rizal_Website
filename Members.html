Rizal's Homecoming and La Liga Filipina

Homecoming & La Liga Members

Dr. Jose Rizal

A Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary who is considered the national hero of the Philippines. He was a polymath, fluent in multiple languages, and authored significant works such as "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," which highlighted the social injustices under Spanish rule. His advocacy for peaceful reforms and education played a crucial role in the country's push towards independence.

Ambrosio Salvador

A Filipino reformist and one of the early leaders of La Liga Filipina. He played a significant role in the organization, supporting its goals of mutual protection, defense against injustice, and the promotion of social reforms.

Deodato Arellano

A Filipino revolutionary and a founding member of La Liga Filipina. He served as its first president and later became involved in the Katipunan, an organization that sought Philippine independence through revolution. Arellano was instrumental in organizing and mobilizing support for the reformist and revolutionary movements.

Bonifacio Arevalo

A Filipino dentist, artist, and nationalist. He was an active member of La Liga Filipina and known for his contributions to the nationalist cause. Arevalo's involvement in the organization highlighted his commitment to the social and political reforms advocated by Rizal and his contemporaries.

Doroteo Ongjunco

A Filipino reformist who hosted the founding meeting of La Liga Filipina at his house in Tondo, Manila. His support for the organization demonstrated his commitment to the reformist cause and the improvement of Filipino society under Spanish rule.

Timoteo Paez

A Filipino reformist and an active member of La Liga Filipina. He participated in the foundational activities of the organization and supported its objectives of mutual aid, protection, and social reform. Paez's involvement underscored his dedication to the movement for peaceful change in the Philippines.
